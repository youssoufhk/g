## 3. Anti-list: funds to NOT pitch

Do not waste warm intros on these.

- **Pure enterprise SaaS funds** (Accel, Index, Sequoia EU): too late-stage for a seed round at our size. Pitch them at Series A.
- **Consumer-focused VCs**: wrong thesis.
- **Silicon Valley funds** without EU presence: hard to get attention, usually want you to move to SF.
- **Funds with portfolio companies that directly compete** (e.g., any fund that owns Kantata, Personio, Lucca, HiBob, Factorial at significant cap table percentage).
- **Funds under €30M AUM**: check size too small, insufficient follow-on capacity.
- **Family offices without SaaS experience**: they will ask the wrong questions and slow the round.

## 4. Warm intro priority list (fill from your network)

Before doing any cold outreach to investors, exhaust warm intros. Priority order:

1. **Former customers of successful European SaaS founders**: ask the founder for a warm intro to their former investors
2. **Consultancy leaders in your existing network**: if they know any of the fund partners personally, request an intro
3. **LinkedIn second-degree connections** at the target funds: use LinkedIn Sales Navigator to find connections, then ask your mutual for an intro
4. **Accelerator alumni**: if either founder has a connection to Y Combinator, Techstars, Founders Factory, Seedcamp alumni, use it
5. **Startup community events**: StartupGrind, SaaStock, European Tech Week, Slush, France Digitale. Conference lists are on each event's site.

**Rule:** never cold-DM a partner at a fund before exhausting warm intros. Cold DMs to investors have a < 2% response rate and burn the first impression.

## 5. Pitch deck prep (do this at customer 3, not before)

- [ ] 10-12 slide deck: problem, solution, market, traction, business model, team, roadmap, ask
- [ ] 3-minute founder video demonstrating the month-end close agent with real customer data
- [ ] Financial model: current burn, 18-month forecast, unit economics, expected ARR trajectory
- [ ] Data room: customer contracts (redacted), customer interviews (video or transcript), product screenshots, architecture overview
- [ ] One-pager: elevator pitch + key numbers + ask + contact

**Template references:** Y Combinator pitch deck guide, Sequoia pitch deck template, Peter Thiel's "question framework" for category-creating startups.

## 6. Fundraise sprint discipline (once triggered)

When the runway hits < 12 months or customer 5 signs:

- [ ] Both founders allocate 30% of weekly time to fundraise (~12 hours each per week)
- [ ] Target: 5 first meetings per week, 2 second meetings per week
- [ ] Total funnel: 40 first meetings → 15 second meetings → 5 partner meetings → 2 term sheets → 1 closed round
- [ ] Expected duration: 8-12 weeks from first meeting to money in the bank
- [ ] Do NOT pause Phase 5 build during fundraise unless runway drops below 6 months (hard stop trigger)
- [ ] Weekly update to the co-founder on fundraise progress, stored in `investors.local.md`
