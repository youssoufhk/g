## 1. Pipeline stages (from `docs/GO_TO_MARKET.md` §4)

| Stage | Definition | Typical time in stage |
|---|---|---:|
| **Curious** | Responded to outreach, aware of Gamma, no call booked | 1-2 weeks |
| **Interested** | Completed a 30-min discovery call, pain confirmed, will take a follow-up call | 2-4 weeks |
| **Evaluating** | Pilot kicked off with real data, weekly check-ins running | 4-8 weeks (the 60-day pilot) |
| **Committed** | Signed non-binding pilot LOI, OR committed in writing to a paid pilot, OR prepaid a deposit | 1-4 weeks to live |
| **Live** | Annual contract signed, production use | ongoing |

Target for the 20-week validated-lead gate: **3 firms at Committed or better**, **10 firms at Interested or better**.

## 2. Outreach message template

Copy this, personalize the bracketed fields, send via LinkedIn DM or email:

```
Hi [First name],

I noticed you lead [specific role / function] at [Firm name]. I'm [Your name], co-founder of Gamma, a new operations platform for consulting firms 50 to 500 employees in France and the UK. We're in early customer discovery and I'd value 30 minutes of your time.

No pitch, no demo. I want to hear how your team handles month-end invoicing, timesheet approval, and client billing today. What slows you down, where you waste hours, where you wish the tool did more. Your answers will directly shape what we build.

Would next Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday afternoon work for a call? Video or phone, whichever is easier for you.

Thanks,
[Your name]
Co-founder, Gamma | Global Gamma Ltd
[LinkedIn profile URL]
```

**Variations (use sparingly):**

- **If you have a mutual connection:** open with "[Mutual's name] suggested I reach out about operations at [Firm]." Replaces the first sentence.
- **If you saw them speak or post:** open with "Saw your talk/post on [topic] last week." Replaces the first sentence. Must be real.
- **If they are a referral from an existing pilot:** "Claire at [Firm1] mentioned you might share similar pain." Highest conversion rate.

**Do NOT:**

- Use the word "disrupting"
- Use the word "game-changing"
- Include a pitch deck link on first contact
- Send before 08:00 or after 18:00 their local time
- Send on Monday morning or Friday afternoon
- Send the same message twice to the same person (one follow-up max, 7 days after)

10. **CFO at a UK Tier 2 consulting firm, 200-400 employees.** Stretch target for the week. Filter: Industry = Management Consulting, Headcount = 201-500, Country = UK, Title = "CFO". Larger firms have longer sales cycles but higher ACV.

**Realistic conversion math for the first 10:**

- 10 cold messages → **1-2 responses** (10-20% response rate)
- 1-2 responses → **0-1 discovery call booked**
- First committed lead typically at message **#50 to #80**

Do not be discouraged by low early response rates. The game is sustained outreach, not first-send genius.

## 5. Discovery call preparation checklist (per call)

- [ ] **T-24h**: confirm the call time via email, send a Google Meet or Zoom link, set a reminder
- [ ] **T-30min**: read the prospect's LinkedIn profile top to bottom, their last 3 public posts, their company website "about" page
- [ ] **T-15min**: re-read the canonical one-liner from `docs/GO_TO_MARKET.md` §1 and the 3 core questions in §6 below
- [ ] **T-5min**: close all other tabs, silence notifications, open a fresh notes file `docs/founder/discovery-notes/YYYY-MM-DD-<firm>.md`
- [ ] **During the call**: 70% listening, 30% talking. No product pitch unless they ask. Write everything down verbatim where possible.
- [ ] **T+2h**: write up notes and decide the next action (follow-up email, schedule second call, move to Interested, archive as not-fit)
- [ ] **T+1 day**: send the follow-up email thanking them and naming the next action

## 6. The 3 questions every discovery call MUST answer

Write these on a post-it next to your laptop. Do not close the call until you have an answer to each.

1. **"Walk me through your last month-end close. Who did what, how long did it take, what went wrong?"** Reveals current pain, current tooling, internal process.
2. **"If Gamma saved your team 4 hours per month on month-end close, what would that be worth to your firm?"** Reveals pricing anchor and value perception.
3. **"What tool would Gamma replace if we nailed the experience you described?"** Reveals the competitive set and the buying persona.

**If you only get 2 of 3 answers**, the call was still successful. If you get 0 of 3, the call was a monologue and the prospect is not ready to discuss.

## 7. Weekly target (sustain over 20 weeks)

- 10-15 new cold outreaches per week
- 2-3 follow-ups on previous weeks' non-responders
- 1-2 warm intro requests through the existing network
- 2-3 discovery calls completed
- 1 outbound per day on average, measured over 5 business days
- No outreach on Saturdays and Sundays

**Realistic week-by-week cumulative targets:**

| Week | Cumulative outreaches | Responses (at 12%) | Calls (at 40% of responses) | Interested (at 50% of calls) | Committed (at 30% of Interested) |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 4 | 50 | 6 | 2 | 1 | 0 |
| 8 | 100 | 12 | 5 | 2 | 1 |
| 12 | 150 | 18 | 7 | 4 | 1 |
| 16 | 200 | 24 | 10 | 5 | 2 |
| **20** | **250** | **30** | **12** | **6** | **3** (gate cleared) |

**The math says:** to clear the validated-lead gate at week 20, you need to sustain ~12-13 outreaches per week starting week 1. Starting week 5 means you need 16-17 per week. Starting week 10 means the gate is mathematically unlikely.

**Start this week. Not next week. This week.**
